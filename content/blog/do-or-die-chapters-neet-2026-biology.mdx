# Do or Die Chapters for NEET 2026 Biology

Not all chapters are created equal in NEET. Some chapters consistently contribute 8-10 questions while others give just 1-2. This guide identifies the "Do or Die" chapters that you MUST master to score 340+ in Biology.

## What Makes a Chapter "Do or Die"?

| Criteria            | Importance                     |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| High Weightage      | 5+ questions every year        |
| Predictable Pattern | Similar questions repeated     |
| NCERT-Heavy         | Direct questions from textbook |
| Scoring Potential   | Easy to moderate difficulty    |
| Time Efficiency     | High marks for time invested   |

---

## The Ultimate Do or Die List for NEET 2026

### Tier 1: Absolute Must-Master (35-40 Questions)

These chapters give 35-40 questions EVERY year. Missing these = missing NEET.

#### 1. Genetics: Principles of Inheritance (8-10 Questions)

**Why It's Do or Die:**

- Highest individual chapter weightage
- Predictable question patterns
- Both numerical and conceptual questions

**Must-Know Topics:**
| Topic | Question Type | Difficulty |
|-------|--------------|------------|
| Mendel's Laws | Ratios, Crosses | Easy |
| Incomplete Dominance | ABO blood groups | Easy |
| Codominance | Example-based | Easy |
| Linkage & Crossing Over | Numerical | Medium |
| Sex Determination | XY, ZW systems | Easy |
| Pedigree Analysis | Family trees | Medium |
| Chromosomal Disorders | Syndromes | Easy |

**Guaranteed Questions:**

- Monohybrid ratio calculations
- Dihybrid cross outcomes
- Blood group inheritance
- Pedigree interpretation

#### 2. Molecular Basis of Inheritance (6-8 Questions)

**Why It's Do or Die:**

- Central dogma is NEET favorite
- Operon concept asked every year
- Diagrams frequently tested

**Must-Know Topics:**
| Topic | Focus Area |
|-------|------------|
| DNA Structure | Watson-Crick model, Chargaff's rule |
| DNA Replication | Enzymes, Semiconservative |
| Transcription | Process in prokaryotes, eukaryotes |
| Translation | Codons, tRNA, Ribosomes |
| Lac Operon | Induction mechanism |
| Gene Regulation | Positive, Negative |
| Human Genome Project | Key facts |

#### 3. Human Physiology (14-16 Questions Combined)

All 7 chapters of Human Physiology together contribute the MOST questions.

**Digestion and Absorption (2-3 Q)**

- Digestive enzymes and their sources
- Absorption mechanisms
- GI tract hormones

**Breathing and Exchange of Gases (2-3 Q)**

- Respiratory volumes
- Gas transport mechanisms
- Oxygen dissociation curve

**Body Fluids and Circulation (3-4 Q)**

- Blood composition
- Heart anatomy and cardiac cycle
- ECG interpretation
- Blood groups and transfusion

**Excretory Products (2-3 Q)**

- Nephron structure
- Urine formation (GFR, tubular secretion)
- Regulation by hormones

**Neural Control (3-4 Q)**

- Brain structure and functions
- Reflex arc
- Synapse transmission
- Eye and Ear structure

**Chemical Coordination (2-3 Q)**

- Endocrine glands and hormones
- Feedback mechanisms
- Disorders of hormones

#### 4. Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants (4-5 Questions)

**Critical Topics:**
| Topic | Importance |
|-------|------------|
| Microsporogenesis | Structure of pollen |
| Megasporogenesis | Embryo sac development |
| Pollination | Types and agents |
| Double Fertilization | Process and significance |
| Post-fertilization | Embryo, Endosperm development |
| Apomixis | Types and examples |

#### 5. Human Reproduction (4-5 Questions)

**Never-Miss Topics:**

- Male and female reproductive systems
- Spermatogenesis and Oogenesis
- Menstrual cycle phases
- Fertilization and implantation
- Pregnancy and parturition
- Lactation

### Tier 2: High-Yield Chapters (25-30 Questions)

#### 6. Cell: The Unit of Life (3-4 Questions)

**Focus On:**

- Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic cells
- Cell organelles and functions
- Nucleus structure
- Endomembrane system

#### 7. Cell Cycle and Cell Division (3-4 Questions)

**Must-Know:**

- Phases of cell cycle
- Mitosis stages and significance
- Meiosis I and II differences
- Checkpoints

#### 8. Biomolecules (3-4 Questions)

**High-Yield Topics:**

- Carbohydrate types
- Amino acids and proteins
- Enzymes - properties and types
- Nucleic acids structure

#### 9. Plant Kingdom (3-4 Questions)

**Don't Skip:**

- Algae classification
- Bryophytes characteristics
- Pteridophytes features
- Gymnosperm examples
- Angiosperm classification

#### 10. Animal Kingdom (4-5 Questions)

**Focus Areas:**

- Basis of classification
- Non-chordates (Phylum-wise)
- Chordates (Class-wise)
- Key examples for each

#### 11. Photosynthesis (3-4 Questions)

**Critical Concepts:**

- Light reactions (PS I, PS II)
- Calvin cycle
- C3 vs C4 vs CAM
- Factors affecting photosynthesis

#### 12. Ecology (6-8 Questions Combined)

**Organisms and Populations (2-3 Q)**

- Population attributes
- Growth models
- Population interactions

**Ecosystem (2-3 Q)**

- Energy flow
- Food chains and webs
- Ecological pyramids
- Nutrient cycling

**Biodiversity (1-2 Q)**

- Types and patterns
- Hotspots
- Conservation strategies

**Environmental Issues (1-2 Q)**

- Pollution types
- Greenhouse effect
- Ozone depletion

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I score 340+ by studying only these 15 chapters?

These chapters contribute 70-75 questions. Mastering them guarantees 280-300 marks. For 340+, you need basic coverage of remaining chapters too, but priority should be these 15.

### Which chapter should I start with?

Start with Human Physiology - it's the largest contributor and relatively easy to understand. Then move to Genetics.

### How deep should I study these chapters?

For these 15 chapters, study NCERT line by line. Every sentence can become a question. Don't skip any example or diagram.

### What if I'm weak in Genetics?

Genetics is non-negotiable for NEET. Take extra time, solve 100+ numericals, watch video explanations. It's worth the investment.
