# How to Choose a USABO Coach or Program: 7 Questions to Ask

You have decided you want USABO coaching. Good — but "USABO coaching" is not a regulated label, and the quality range is enormous, from serious olympiad programs to general test-prep tutors who added "USABO" to their menu. Before you pay anyone, ask these seven questions. The answers tell you almost everything.

## 1. Who actually teaches — and how deep is their biology?

USABO is university-level biology. A tutor who is strong at SAT or general science is not automatically a USABO coach. Ask directly: **what is the instructor's biology background, and have they taught olympiad-level material specifically?** You want faculty whose subject depth clearly exceeds the Open and Semifinal, not someone one chapter ahead of the student.

> At Cerebrum, USABO is taught by AIIMS-trained, biology-only faculty — we teach one subject, at depth.

## 2. Live faculty-led classes, or a video library?

There is a big difference between **learning from a teacher in real time** and **watching pre-recorded videos**. Recorded content is fine as a supplement, but it cannot answer your question, cannot see where you are stuck, and cannot adapt. Ask: **are classes live, and is it the same instructor throughout the year?** A fixed instructor who knows your weaknesses is worth far more than a rotating cast or a passive playlist.

## 3. Do they give written feedback on every past-paper attempt?

This is the single highest-signal question. Improvement on USABO comes from the **error-review loop** — attempting past papers, then having a knowledgeable teacher analyze every wrong answer and show you the pattern you keep missing. Many programs assign past papers; few actually _read and mark_ your attempts. Ask: **when I do a past paper, does a teacher review my specific errors and tell me what to fix?** If the answer is vague, keep looking.

## 4. What is the batch size?

Olympiad coaching is not a lecture hall. In a batch of 40, no one is reviewing your individual work. Ask for the **actual maximum batch size**, and whether 1:1 is available. Small batches (single digits to low double digits) or 1:1 are where the feedback loop in question 3 is even possible.

> Cerebrum runs small live batches (max ~12) plus 1:1 options — small enough that faculty read every student's past-paper work.

## 5. Does the schedule fit your time zone and your AP load?

If you are in the US and the coach is abroad, ask whether classes run in **US-friendly evening time zones (ET / CT / MT / PT)** — not the middle of your school night. And ask whether the program is built to run **in parallel with AP Biology**, since the smartest students prepare for both at once (February USABO precedes the May AP exam, and the prep overlaps heavily).

## 6. Do they prepare you for the Semifinal — including the practical side?

Many programs stop at the Open Exam. But the Open is just the entry point; the **Semifinal is a different, harder exam** (longer, with select-all and short-response formats and primary-research-style data interpretation), and the National Finals include a **lab/practical component**. Ask: **does the program cover Semifinal strategy and, for serious contenders, practical/lab skills — or only Open-level content?**

## 7. What do they actually claim — and can they back it?

Ask about track record, but listen for **how** they answer. Honest programs describe their approach and are specific and measured about outcomes. Be wary of vague grandeur and **run from anyone promising "guaranteed selection," a guaranteed Semifinal slot, or a fabricated statistic** ("X% of our students get into MIT"). USABO is genuinely competitive; no one can guarantee a result, and in many jurisdictions advertising a guaranteed competitive outcome is not even permitted. A coach willing to be honest about what they can and cannot promise is the one you want.

## A quick red-flag checklist

Walk away if a program:

- Cannot name the instructor's specific biology credentials.
- Is only pre-recorded video with no live teaching.
- Does not review your individual past-paper errors.
- Hides its batch size or runs huge classes.
- Only teaches to the Open Exam.
- Promises "guaranteed selection" or cites an unsourced admit-rate statistic.

## The bottom line

The seven questions come down to one idea: **a real USABO program is deep, live, small, and honest.** It is taught by genuine biology specialists, in real time, in small groups, with a working error-review loop, all the way through the Semifinal — and it tells you the truth about what it can deliver.
