# NEET Cut-off 2026: Complete Category-wise Guide

Understanding NEET cut-off is crucial for realistic goal-setting and college planning. This comprehensive guide covers everything about NEET 2026 cut-off - from qualifying percentile to admission cut-off for top medical colleges.

---

## Types of NEET Cut-off

Before diving into numbers, understand there are **two types of cut-off**:

### 1. Qualifying Cut-off (NTA)

- Minimum marks/percentile to be **eligible for counselling**
- Set by NTA (National Testing Agency)
- Different for each category
- Does NOT guarantee admission

### 2. Admission Cut-off

- Actual marks at which **admission closes** for a college
- Varies by college, category, and quota
- Changes every year based on competition
- What actually determines your college

### Why Cut-off Changes Every Year

| -------------------- | ----------------------------------- |

## Understanding AIQ vs State Quota

### 15% AIQ Seats

| Aspect        | Details                             |
| ------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Controlled by | MCC (Medical Counselling Committee) |
| Eligibility   | All India candidates                |
| Cut-off       | Generally higher                    |
| Advantage     | Access to all states                |
| Process       | Online, single window               |

### 85% State Quota Seats

| Aspect        | Details                     |
| ------------- | --------------------------- |
| Controlled by | State counselling authority |
| Eligibility   | State domicile required     |
| Cut-off       | Lower for home state        |
| Advantage     | More seats available        |
| Process       | State-specific              |

### Strategy: AIQ or State?

| If your score is... | Strategy                           |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Very high (680+)    | Try AIQ first for top AIIMS        |
| High (620-680)      | Compare both, choose better option |
| Moderate (550-620)  | State quota usually better         |
| Low (450-550)       | Definitely state quota             |

## Special Category Information

### EWS (Economically Weaker Section)

**Eligibility:**

- Family income < ₹8 lakhs per annum
- Don't own more than 5 acres agricultural land
- Residential house < 1000 sq ft (urban) / 100 sq yards (town)
- Not covered under SC/ST/OBC reservation

**Seats:** 10% horizontal reservation

### PwD (Persons with Disability)

**Eligibility:**

- Minimum 40% disability
- Certificate from authorized medical board

**Relaxation:**

- Lower qualifying percentile
- 5% horizontal reservation
- Relaxed admission cut-off

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the expected NEET 2026 cut-off for General category?

The qualifying cut-off is expected to be around 50th percentile (approximately 137-150 marks). For admission to top GMCs, you'll need 650+ marks.

### Can I get a government medical college with 550 marks?

Yes, if you belong to SC/ST category or have strong state quota preference. General category candidates may find it difficult at AIQ but might get options in state quota of less competitive states.

### Is EWS quota easier than General?

The qualifying cut-off is same as General, but competition is relatively less in EWS quota. You have better chances of getting good colleges at the same score.

### What score is needed for AIIMS Delhi?

For AIIMS Delhi General category, you typically need rank under 50, which translates to 715+ marks. For reserved categories, the requirement is lower.

### How much does cut-off drop in Round 2?

Typically, cut-off drops by 5-15 marks in Round 2 compared to Round 1. However, top colleges rarely see significant drops.
