# How to Register for USABO: Step-by-Step

The most common USABO registration mistake is looking for a "register as a student" button. There isn't one — and understanding why is the whole key. **USABO registration is school-based.** Here is exactly how it works, and what to do in the tricky cases.

> ⚠️ **Registration dates and fees change every cycle.** The mechanics below are stable, but always confirm the **current deadline and fee** on the official sources — [cee.org](https://www.cee.org) and [usabo-trc.org](https://www.usabo-trc.org) — before you act. Specific dates in this guide are examples from a recent cycle, not a promise for yours.

## The core rule: schools register, not students

You do not sign yourself up for USABO. Your **school registers with the Center for Excellence in Education (CEE)**, pays the school registration fee, and is then able to enrol its students to sit the Open Exam. A recent cycle's fee has been about **$125 per school**, which covers all of that school's students — but confirm the current figure, because it can change.

So the first question is never "how do I register?" — it is **"is my school registered, and if not, who can register it?"**

## Step by step (the normal case)

1. **Check whether your school already participates.** Ask your biology teacher, science-department head, or a science-olympiad coordinator. Many schools with strong science programmes register every year.
2. **If it does, ask to be enrolled.** The school's USABO coordinator adds you to the roster for the Open Exam. You may also need to complete a linked account (in recent cycles, students link to an Art of Problem Solving account) — the coordinator will point you to the current step.
3. **Confirm the Open Exam logistics.** The Open is a proctored exam (recent format: 50 multiple-choice questions in 50 minutes, online) held in **February**. Your school proctors it.
4. **Watch the deadline.** School registration typically opens in the autumn and **closes before the February Open** — commonly around early November in recent cycles. Verify your cycle's exact date.

## If your school is NOT participating

This is the case that panics families, and it has real solutions:

- **Ask a teacher to register the school.** The most direct fix. Registration is not onerous for a teacher, and one registration covers every interested student at the school — so you are helping your classmates too. Bring them the official CEE registration link.
- **Arrange a host school.** If your own school genuinely will not register, students can sometimes sit the exam through another **registered ("host") school** in the area. Ask around local schools with science-olympiad programmes, or ask CEE about the current host-school process.
- **Homeschoolers:** homeschooled students are eligible, generally by arranging to test through a local registered school. Check the current official guidance for the exact mechanism.

The one thing that does **not** exist is an individual, last-minute back door once the window closes — which is exactly why you should sort this out in the autumn, not in January.

## A quick timeline to plan around

Roughly, in order (confirm each cycle):

- **Autumn** — school registration opens.
- **Early November (typical)** — registration deadline.
- **February** — Open Exam.
- **Spring** — Semifinal for those who advance.
- **June** — National Finals (recently held at Harvard).

If you want a dated planning calendar, see our [USABO timeline guide](/usabo-timeline-calendar-key-dates) — and remember every date needs confirming against the official site for your year.
