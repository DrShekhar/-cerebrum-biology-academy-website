## The Biggest Mistake NEET Droppers Make

After counseling 500+ dropper students at Cerebrum Biology Academy, I've identified the **#1 reason droppers fail again**:

### They Join the Same Type of Mass Coaching That Failed Them Before

Think about it. If your first attempt didn't work with:
- Large batch sizes (100-1000+ students)
- No personal attention
- Generic study plans
- Delayed doubt clearing
- No analysis of what went wrong

**Why would the same approach work in your drop year?**

### Common Mistakes to Avoid in Dropper Year

#### 1. Choosing Mass Coaching Programs

**Red Flags:**
- Batch sizes of 1000+ students online (platforms like PW, Unacademy)
- Batch sizes of 100+ students offline (institutes like Allen, Aakash)
- No assigned personal mentor who tracks YOUR progress
- Generic study plan sent to all students
- Doubt clearing takes 24-48 hours (or never happens)

**Why This Fails:**
- You're just a number, not a student
- Your specific weak areas are never addressed
- You follow the same approach that failed you
- No emotional support during tough phases
- Parents have no visibility into your progress

#### 2. Not Analyzing First Attempt Failures

**The Reality:** 80% of droppers never do a proper gap analysis of their first attempt.

They jump into studying without understanding:
- Which chapters cost them the most marks?
- Was it conceptual weakness or silly mistakes?
- Did they lack practice or lack clarity?
- What was their time management like?

**Result:** They repeat the same mistakes and fail again.

#### 3. Following One-Size-Fits-All Study Plans

Your friend who scored 520 needs a different plan than you who scored 420. But mass coaching gives everyone the same schedule.

**What you actually need:**
- Customized plan based on YOUR first attempt analysis
- More time on YOUR weak chapters
- Practice intensity matched to YOUR level
- Flexibility to adjust based on YOUR progress
