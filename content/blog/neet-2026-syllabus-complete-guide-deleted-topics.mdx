# NEET 2026 Syllabus: Complete Official Guide with All Changes

The National Testing Agency (NTA) has released the official NEET 2026 syllabus with several important changes. This comprehensive guide covers every deleted topic, newly emphasized area, and chapter-wise weightage to help you prepare strategically.

| Aspect            | Details                       |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------- |

## Major Syllabus Changes in NEET 2026

### Deleted Topics from Biology

The following topics have been **removed** from NEET 2026 syllabus:

#### Class 11 Biology - Deleted Topics

| Unit                      | Deleted Topics                                                |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Diversity in Living World | Taxonomical aids (details), Lichens (detailed study)          |
| Structural Organisation   | Earthworm morphology (detailed), Cockroach anatomy (diagrams) |
| Cell Structure            | Detailed ultrastructure of cell organelles                    |
| Plant Physiology          | Kranz anatomy details, C4 pathway mechanism                   |
| Human Physiology          | Details of disorders (only names required)                    |

#### Class 12 Biology - Deleted Topics

| Unit                     | Deleted Topics                                  |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Reproduction             | Detailed embryo development stages              |
| Genetics                 | Hardy-Weinberg principle (derivation)           |
| Biology in Human Welfare | Detailed drug abuse mechanisms                  |
| Biotechnology            | Detailed cloning vectors                        |
| Ecology                  | Mathematical calculations in population ecology |

### Newly Emphasized Topics

These topics have **increased importance** in NEET 2026:

1. **Molecular Biology** - DNA replication, transcription, translation
2. **Human Physiology** - All systems with clinical correlations
3. **Genetics** - Mendelian inheritance, chromosomal disorders
4. **Ecology** - Environmental issues, biodiversity conservation
5. **Plant Physiology** - Photosynthesis, respiration basics

## Expert Preparation Strategy for NEET 2026

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-4)

- Complete NCERT Class 11 & 12 thoroughly
- Make notes of important diagrams
- Focus on understanding concepts, not memorization

### Phase 2: Deep Learning (Months 5-8)

- Solve chapter-wise PYQs
- Take topic-wise tests
- Revise weak areas

### Phase 3: Revision & Practice (Months 9-12)

- Multiple NCERT readings
- Full-length mock tests
- Previous year paper analysis

### NCERT is King

> **95% of NEET Biology questions come directly from NCERT textbooks.**

Focus on:

- Reading NCERT line by line
- Understanding diagrams thoroughly
- Memorizing important tables and flowcharts
- Learning scientific names and examples

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are the major changes in NEET 2026 syllabus?

The NEET 2026 syllabus has approximately 12 topics deleted from Biology, reducing overall content by 15%. Hardy-Weinberg derivation, detailed organism studies, and mathematical ecology calculations have been removed.

### Is NCERT enough for NEET 2026 Biology?

Yes, NCERT is sufficient for 90-95% of NEET Biology questions. Complete mastery of NCERT textbooks should be your primary goal.

### Which chapters have highest weightage in NEET Biology?

Genetics (Mendelian + Molecular) has the highest weightage at 18-20%, followed by Human Physiology (15-18%) and Reproduction (12-14%).

### How many questions come from Class 11 vs Class 12?

Approximately 45% questions come from Class 11 and 55% from Class 12, though this varies each year.
