# USABO Timeline & Calendar: Every Stage From Registration to National Finals

The USABO **sequence** never changes; the exact **dates** shift a little every year. If you understand the sequence and plan backward from the February Open Exam, you will never be caught out — and you will avoid the one fatal mistake, which is missing the registration window in the autumn.

> ⚠️ **The stages below are stable; the specific dates are not.** Registration deadlines and the fee change every cycle, so **confirm the exact dates for your year on the official sources — [cee.org](https://www.cee.org) and [usabo-trc.org](https://www.usabo-trc.org)** — before planning around them. Dates shown are typical of recent cycles.

## The sequence, in order

| Stage                     | Typical timing         | What happens                                                                 |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **School registration**   | Autumn                 | Your school registers with CEE and enrols students (recent fee ~$125/school) |
| **Registration deadline** | ~Early November        | The last date a school can register — miss it and there is no back door      |
| **Open Exam**             | February               | Recent format: 50 multiple-choice questions in 50 minutes, online, at school |
| **Semifinal Exam**        | Spring (after grading) | Roughly the top 10% of Open takers advance; longer, harder, free-response    |
| **National Finals**       | June                   | Top ~20 finalists; theory + practical, recently at Harvard University        |
| **Team USA selection**    | At Finals              | The top 4 finalists form the US team                                         |
| **IBO**                   | July                   | International Biology Olympiad; 2026 is in Vilnius, Lithuania (Jul 12-19)    |

## How to plan your study around it

Work **backward from the February Open**:

- **Six months out (previous autumn):** begin structured preparation — Campbell coverage plus the areas AP underweights (plant/animal physiology, ethology, biosystematics). This is also when school registration happens, so handle both together.
- **~Two months out (December–January):** shift to past-paper saturation and timed Open simulations.
- **February:** the Open Exam. Arrive with a full preparation cycle behind you.
- **If you advance:** the spring Semifinal is a different, harder exam (longer, free-response, data interpretation) — the weeks between the Open and Semifinal are for stepping up to that depth.
- **June onward:** for finalists, the National Finals include a practical/lab component; the very top form Team USA for the July IBO.

A student who starts in the autumn arrives at the February Open with real depth. A student who starts in January is racing the clock — it can be done, but it is the harder road.

## The eligibility line on the calendar

One thing that intersects the timeline and trips people up: the **Open Exam is open to any student at a registered US school**, but **advancing past the Open** (Semifinal, Finals, Team USA) requires **US citizenship or permanent residency**. So an international student at a US school can take the February Open regardless of citizenship, but the spring Semifinal and beyond are citizenship-gated.
