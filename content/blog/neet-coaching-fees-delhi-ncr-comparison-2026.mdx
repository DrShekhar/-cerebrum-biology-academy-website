## The Real Cost: What Fees Do Not Tell You

### Hidden costs at large coaching centres

When comparing fees, factor in these commonly overlooked expenses:

- **Registration/admission fee**: Rs 2,000 - Rs 10,000 (non-refundable, charged separately)
- **Study material fee**: Some centres charge Rs 5,000 - Rs 15,000 extra for printed material
- **Test series fee**: Premium test series may cost Rs 3,000 - Rs 8,000 additional
- **Re-admission fee**: If you want to continue for a second year or switch batches
- **Transportation**: Monthly metro pass (Rs 1,500 - Rs 2,500) or auto/cab costs
- **Food**: Students at all-day coaching spend Rs 3,000 - Rs 5,000 monthly on meals

### The fee-per-student calculation

This is the metric that actually matters. Divide the annual fee by the batch size:

| Institute | Fee (1-year) | Batch Size | Fee Per Student Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| Allen | Rs 1,40,000 | 100 | Rs 1,400 |
| Aakash | Rs 1,20,000 | 80 | Rs 1,500 |
| PW Centres | Rs 60,000 | 150 | Rs 400 |
| Cerebrum | Rs 95,000 | 20 | Rs 4,750 |

What does this tell you? Cerebrum charges more per student ratio because you get 4-5x more personal attention. Each student in a Cerebrum batch of 20 gets more faculty time than a student in a batch of 150, even if the absolute fee is lower at the larger institute.

## How to Evaluate Value for Money

### Do not compare absolute fees

A Rs 60,000 program with 150 students per batch and a Rs 1,00,000 program with 25 students per batch are not comparable on price alone. The second gives you 6x more teacher access per rupee spent.

### Calculate cost per effective study hour

**Example calculation:**
- Coaching A: Rs 1,40,000 for 600 class hours = Rs 233 per hour. But with 100 students, your personal interaction time is ~6 hours total over the year.
- Coaching B: Rs 95,000 for 500 class hours = Rs 190 per hour. With 20 students, your personal interaction time is ~25 hours total over the year.

Coaching B costs less per hour AND gives you 4x more personal attention.

### Ask for centre-specific results

National result claims are meaningless for your decision. Ask:
- How many students from this specific centre cleared NEET last year?
- What was the average score of students in this specific batch?
- How many students were in the batch? (Divide selections by batch size for the real success rate.)
