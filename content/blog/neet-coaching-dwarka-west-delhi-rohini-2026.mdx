West and North Delhi have emerged as serious contenders to the traditional coaching belt of South Delhi and Rajinder Nagar. Areas like Dwarka, Rohini, Pitampura, and Janakpuri now host branches of every major coaching chain alongside strong independent institutes.

For families in these parts of Delhi, the question is no longer whether good NEET coaching exists nearby. It does. The question is how to pick the right one.

## Dwarka — The West Delhi Coaching Hub

Dwarka is one of Delhi's largest residential sub-cities, and its student population supports a thriving coaching ecosystem. Sectors 4 through 14 along the Blue Line metro corridor have the highest concentration of coaching centres.

### Why Dwarka works for NEET students

- **Metro connectivity**: The Blue Line runs through the heart of Dwarka, with stations at Sector 9, Sector 10, Sector 11, Sector 12, Sector 14, and Sector 21. Students from Najafgarh, Gurgaon (via Huda City Centre interchange), and Central Delhi can reach Dwarka in under 40 minutes.
- **Affordable living**: Students from outside Delhi find Dwarka's PG and hostel options more affordable than South Delhi.
- **School density**: Major schools like Mount Abu, Bal Bharati, DAV, and Delhi International School feed students into nearby coaching.

### Coaching options in Dwarka

- **National franchises**: Allen, Aakash, and PW all have Dwarka branches. These operate large batches (80-150 students) and follow standardised curricula.
- **Regional centres**: Several mid-size coaching institutes in Sectors 6 and 12 offer batches of 30-60 students at lower fees.
- **Online/hybrid**: Many Dwarka students combine local coaching with online biology-specialist programs for stronger NEET preparation.

### Limitations to consider

Franchise branches in Dwarka sometimes have junior faculty compared to their flagship centres. Always verify the specific teachers at the Dwarka branch rather than relying on the brand's overall reputation.

## Pitampura and Shalimar Bagh

Pitampura complements Rohini as a coaching destination. The Netaji Subhash Place metro station (Red and Pink Line interchange) is a key transit point. Students from Model Town, GTB Nagar, and Mukherjee Nagar often travel to Pitampura for coaching.

**Key coaching characteristics:**
- Fees tend to be 10-15% lower than South Delhi for equivalent quality
- More options for vernacular-medium (Hindi) coaching
- Good food and stationery markets around NSP for student convenience

## Janakpuri and Vikaspuri

Janakpuri, connected via the Blue Line, serves as a coaching centre for students from Tilak Nagar, Uttam Nagar, Paschim Vihar, and Vikaspuri. While not as dense as Dwarka or Rohini, Janakpuri has a few well-established NEET centres near Janakpuri West metro station.

## How to Choose Between Dwarka and Rohini

The decision often comes down to geography, but here are some factors beyond distance:

### Choose Rohini if:
- You live in North or North-West Delhi (Pitampura, Shalimar Bagh, Model Town, Ashok Vihar)
- You want access to Cerebrum's biology-specialist coaching at the DC Chowk centre
- You prefer a more student-community atmosphere with study groups and libraries nearby
- You are on the Red Line metro corridor

### Choose Dwarka if:
- You live in West or South-West Delhi (Janakpuri, Najafgarh, Palam, Gurgaon border)

## What Parents Should Verify Before Enrolling

1. **Meet the actual faculty** — Not the counsellor, not the branch manager. Meet the teacher who will be in your child's classroom.
2. **Check the specific branch's results** — National results do not reflect branch-level performance. Ask for centre-specific data.
3. **Understand the test schedule** — How often are tests conducted? Is there detailed analytics or just a score?
4. **Ask about doubt resolution** — What happens when a student does not understand a concept? Is there a system beyond "ask after class"?
5. **Evaluate the study material** — Is it NCERT-aligned? Does it include previous year NEET questions mapped by chapter?
