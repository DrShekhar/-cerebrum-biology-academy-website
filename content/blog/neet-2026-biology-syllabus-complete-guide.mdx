# NEET 2026 Biology Syllabus: Complete Guide

The NEET Biology syllabus covers Class 11 and Class 12 NCERT Biology. With 90 questions worth 360 marks, Biology is the highest-scoring subject in NEET. This guide covers every chapter with weightage analysis.

---

## NEET 2026 Exam Pattern - Biology Section

| Parameter         | Details                          |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Total Questions   | 90 (45 Botany + 45 Zoology)      |
| Total Marks       | 360                              |
| Type of Questions | MCQ with 4 options               |
| Marking Scheme    | +4 for correct, -1 for incorrect |
| Time for Biology  | 100 minutes (recommended)        |

---

## Class 11 Biology Syllabus

### Unit 1: Diversity in Living World

| Chapter                   | Topics                                                     | Expected Questions |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| The Living World          | Taxonomy, nomenclature, taxonomic categories               | 1-2                |
| Biological Classification | Five kingdom classification, viruses, viroids              | 2-3                |
| Plant Kingdom             | Algae, bryophytes, pteridophytes, gymnosperms, angiosperms | 2-3                |
| Animal Kingdom            | Classification from Porifera to Chordata                   | 3-4                |

**Total Expected Questions: 8-12**

**High-Yield Topics:**

- Five Kingdom Classification characteristics
- Life cycle patterns in plants
- Characteristic features of each phylum

---

### Unit 2: Structural Organisation in Plants and Animals

| Chapter                            | Topics                                     | Expected Questions |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ------------------ |
| Morphology of Flowering Plants     | Root, stem, leaf, flower, fruit, seed      | 2-3                |
| Anatomy of Flowering Plants        | Tissues, tissue systems, anatomy of organs | 2-3                |
| Structural Organisation in Animals | Animal tissues, organ systems              | 2-3                |

**Total Expected Questions: 6-9**

**High-Yield Topics:**

- Types of tissue systems
- Modifications of root, stem, leaf
- Types of epithelial and connective tissues

---

### Unit 3: Cell Structure and Function

| Chapter                      | Topics                                                         | Expected Questions |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| Cell: The Unit of Life       | Cell theory, prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells, cell organelles | 3-4                |
| Biomolecules                 | Carbohydrates, proteins, lipids, nucleic acids, enzymes        | 3-4                |
| Cell Cycle and Cell Division | Cell cycle, mitosis, meiosis                                   | 2-3                |

**Total Expected Questions: 8-11**

**High-Yield Topics:**

- Structure and function of all organelles
- Enzyme kinetics and properties
- Stages of mitosis and meiosis

---

### Unit 4: Plant Physiology

| Chapter                      | Topics                                           | Expected Questions |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------ |
| Transport in Plants          | Water transport, transpiration, translocation    | 2-3                |
| Mineral Nutrition            | Essential minerals, deficiency symptoms          | 2-3                |
| Photosynthesis               | Light reaction, Calvin cycle, C3 and C4 pathways | 3-4                |
| Respiration                  | Glycolysis, Krebs cycle, ETC, fermentation       | 2-3                |
| Plant Growth and Development | Plant hormones, photoperiodism, vernalization    | 2-3                |

**Total Expected Questions: 11-16**

**High-Yield Topics:**

- Light reaction steps and electron transport
- C3, C4, and CAM pathway differences
- All plant hormone functions

---

### Unit 5: Human Physiology

| Chapter                               | Topics                                      | Expected Questions |
| ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| Digestion and Absorption              | Digestive system, enzymes, disorders        | 2-3                |
| Breathing and Exchange of Gases       | Respiratory system, gas exchange, disorders | 2-3                |
| Body Fluids and Circulation           | Blood, heart, circulation, disorders        | 3-4                |
| Excretory Products and Elimination    | Nephron, urine formation, disorders         | 2-3                |
| Locomotion and Movement               | Muscles, skeleton, joints, disorders        | 2-3                |
| Neural Control and Coordination       | Nervous system, reflex arc, sensory organs  | 2-3                |
| Chemical Coordination and Integration | Endocrine glands, hormones, disorders       | 3-4                |

**Total Expected Questions: 16-23**

**High-Yield Topics:**

- Complete hormonal functions with disorders
- Cardiac cycle and ECG
- Nephron structure and urine formation

---

## Class 12 Biology Syllabus

### Unit 6: Reproduction

| Chapter                                 | Topics                                              | Expected Questions |
| --------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| Reproduction in Organisms               | Asexual and sexual reproduction                     | 1-2                |
| Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants | Flower structure, pollination, fertilization        | 3-4                |
| Human Reproduction                      | Male and female reproductive systems, gametogenesis | 3-4                |
| Reproductive Health                     | Birth control, STDs, infertility                    | 2-3                |

**Total Expected Questions: 9-13**

---

### Unit 7: Genetics and Evolution

| Chapter                                 | Topics                                                 | Expected Questions |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------ |
| Principles of Inheritance and Variation | Mendelian genetics, gene interaction                   | 4-5                |
| Molecular Basis of Inheritance          | DNA structure, replication, transcription, translation | 4-5                |
| Evolution                               | Origin of life, theories of evolution, human evolution | 2-3                |

**Total Expected Questions: 10-13**

**High-Yield Topics:**

- All types of Mendelian crosses
- DNA replication enzymes
- Hardy-Weinberg principle

---

### Unit 8: Biology and Human Welfare

| Chapter                                       | Topics                                  | Expected Questions |
| --------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| Human Health and Disease                      | Common diseases, immunity, AIDS, cancer | 3-4                |
| Strategies for Enhancement in Food Production | Animal husbandry, plant breeding        | 2-3                |
| Microbes in Human Welfare                     | Microbes in food, medicine, biogas      | 2-3                |

**Total Expected Questions: 7-10**

---

### Unit 9: Biotechnology

| Chapter                                 | Topics                                     | Expected Questions |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ------------------ |
| Biotechnology: Principles and Processes | rDNA technology, tools, vectors            | 3-4                |
| Biotechnology and Its Applications      | GM crops, gene therapy, transgenic animals | 2-3                |

**Total Expected Questions: 5-7**

---

### Unit 10: Ecology and Environment

| Chapter                       | Topics                                               | Expected Questions |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| Organisms and Populations     | Populations, adaptations                             | 2-3                |
| Ecosystem                     | Ecosystem components, productivity, nutrient cycling | 3-4                |
| Biodiversity and Conservation | Biodiversity, conservation strategies                | 2-3                |
| Environmental Issues          | Pollution, conservation, global warming              | 2-3                |

**Total Expected Questions: 9-13**

**High-Yield Topics:**

- All ecological pyramids
- Biogeochemical cycles
- Biodiversity hotspots

## Preparation Strategy by Unit

### Phase 1 (Month 1-3): Foundation

1. Complete Class 11 syllabus
2. Focus on NCERT thoroughly
3. Make short notes for each chapter

### Phase 2 (Month 4-6): Advanced

1. Complete Class 12 syllabus
2. Start solving previous year questions
3. Identify weak chapters

### Phase 3 (Month 7-9): Practice

1. Solve full-length mock tests
2. Revise high-weightage chapters
3. Focus on diagrams

### Phase 4 (Month 10-12): Final Revision

1. Revise short notes daily
2. Only mock tests and analysis
3. Maintain question practice
