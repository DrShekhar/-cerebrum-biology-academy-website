## The 10 Factors That Actually Matter

### Factor 1: Batch Size

This is the single most important factor that parents overlook. Here is the reality:

| Batch Size | Individual Attention | Doubt Resolution | Result Quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| 100-200 students | Almost none | Raise hand and hope | Low success rate |
| 50-80 students | Minimal | Post-class queue | Moderate |
| 25-30 students | Good | Same-day resolution | Strong |
| 12-15 students | Excellent | Immediate, in-class | Highest success rate |

At Cerebrum Biology Academy, our batch sizes range from 12 (Pinnacle) to 25 (Pursuit). The teacher knows every student's strengths, weaknesses, and progress trajectory by name.

### Factor 2: Faculty Credentials

Ask specific questions:
- What is the faculty member's educational background? (Medical degree, PhD, or MSc?)
- How many years have they taught NEET specifically?
- Do they teach the entire syllabus or only selected chapters?
- Can you sit in on a live class before enrolling?

Red flag: If the institute only shows you a marketing presentation but will not let you meet the actual teacher, walk away.

### Factor 3: Verifiable Results

"95% success rate" means nothing without verifiable data. Ask for:
- Student names and their NEET scores
- Which medical colleges they were admitted to
- Contact details of past students or parents who can confirm

At Cerebrum, our 247 AIIMS selections and 98% success rate are backed by named students with documented scores.

### Factor 4: Study Material Quality

NEET Biology is 95% NCERT-based. Good study material should:
- Supplement NCERT, not replace it
- Include chapter-wise MCQ banks from previous years
- Provide topic-wise difficulty grading
- Be regularly updated with the latest exam patterns

Avoid institutes that push thick proprietary textbooks. If the material contradicts NCERT or goes far beyond it, the approach is wrong.

### Factor 5: Mock Test Infrastructure

Mock tests are where NEET preparation actually solidifies. Evaluate:
- How many full-length mock tests are conducted per year? (50+ is ideal)
- Are they timed and conducted under exam conditions?
- Is detailed performance analytics provided after each test?
- Can students take tests at the center and from home?

### Factor 6: Commute Time

This factor is underestimated. A 45-minute one-way commute means 1.5 hours lost daily — that is over 500 hours across two years. Those hours could fund 200+ additional practice sessions.

**Ideal commute: Under 30 minutes one way.** This is why having multiple center locations matters.

### Factor 7: Online + Offline Flexibility

Life is unpredictable. School exams, family events, illness, bad weather — any of these can disrupt a rigid offline schedule. A good institute offers:
- Seamless switching between offline and online modes
- Recorded lectures for missed classes
- Online mock tests with proctoring
- No penalty for switching modes

### Factor 8: Doubt Resolution Speed

The time between encountering a doubt and resolving it directly impacts learning. Compare:

| Model | Doubt Resolution Time | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Large batch, post-class queue | 1-3 days | Student moves on, gap compounds |
| WhatsApp group | 12-24 hours | Acceptable but impersonal |
| Small batch, in-class | Immediate | Best learning outcome |
| 1-on-1 mentor session | Same day, scheduled | Ideal for complex doubts |

### Factor 9: Parent Communication

You are investing significant money and your child's future. The institute should provide:
- Regular progress reports (at least quarterly)
- Parent-teacher meetings
- Transparent communication about attendance and performance
- Access to mock test scores and analytics

If the institute takes your money and then goes silent until results day, that is a problem.

### Factor 10: Fee Transparency

Hidden fees are a red flag. Before enrolling, get written clarity on:
- Total annual fee (not just the "starting from" number)
- What is included: study material, mock tests, doubt sessions
- What costs extra: crash courses, revision modules, supplementary material
- Payment schedule and EMI options
- Refund policy if you withdraw early

## The Comparison Checklist

Use this checklist when evaluating any institute (including ours — we welcome the comparison):

- [ ] Batch size confirmed in writing (not "up to")
- [ ] Met the actual teaching faculty (not just counselors)
- [ ] Attended a demo class
- [ ] Received fee breakdown with no hidden costs
- [ ] Verified results with student names and scores
- [ ] Understood the makeup class and missed class policy
- [ ] Checked online/hybrid availability
- [ ] Reviewed study material samples
- [ ] Asked about mock test frequency and format
- [ ] Spoken with at least one current parent or student
