## How to Choose the Right Exam Center

### Factors to Consider

| Factor        | Why Important                     | Recommendation         |
| ------------- | --------------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Distance      | Travel stress affects performance | Choose within 50 km    |
| Travel Time   | Early morning travel is tiring    | Maximum 2 hours travel |
| Accommodation | May need to stay overnight        | Book early             |
| Connectivity  | Easy transport access             | Good bus/train/flight  |
| Familiarity   | Knowing area reduces stress       | Prefer known cities    |
| Climate       | Weather affects comfort           | Check May weather      |

### Priority Order for Selection

1. **First Choice:** Nearest city with sufficient centers
2. **Second Choice:** Second nearest city
3. **Third Choice:** State capital (usually more centers)
4. **Fourth Choice:** Neighboring state city if closer

### Common Selection Mistakes

| Mistake                   | Problem                   | Solution                    |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Choosing only home city   | May not get slot          | Add backup options          |
| Selecting random cities   | Difficult travel          | Research all choices        |
| Ignoring capacity         | May be allotted elsewhere | Choose high-capacity cities |
| Not checking connectivity | Travel issues             | Verify transport            |
| Forgetting accommodation  | Stress on exam day        | Book early if far           |

## Center Allotment Process

### How NTA Allots Centers

| Step | Process                                  |
| ---- | ---------------------------------------- |
| 1    | Candidate submits 4 preferences          |
| 2    | System checks availability in 1st choice |
| 3    | If available, center allotted            |
| 4    | If not, moves to 2nd choice              |
| 5    | Process continues to 4th choice          |
| 6    | If none available, nearby city allotted  |

### What If You Get a Different Center?

| Situation                      | Action                   |
| ------------------------------ | ------------------------ |
| Different city in same state   | Plan travel accordingly  |
| Different city in nearby state | Book accommodation       |
| Very far city                  | Contact NTA (rare cases) |

## City-wise Preparation Tips

### Metro Cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad)

**Advantages:**

- Maximum centers available
- Easy accommodation
- Good transport

**Challenges:**

- Traffic on exam day
- Crowded centers
- Finding exact location

**Tips:**

- Leave 2 hours extra for traffic
- Use metro/train if possible
- Pre-visit center location

### Tier 2 Cities

**Advantages:**

- Less traffic
- Smaller centers
- Familiar environment

**Challenges:**

- Fewer centers
- May be allotted elsewhere
- Limited accommodation

**Tips:**

- Book early
- Have backup travel plan
- Choose multiple cities

### Small Towns

**Advantages:**

- Home comfort
- Known locations
- Less stress

**Challenges:**

- Very few centers
- May be sent to nearby city
- Limited capacity

**Tips:**

- Choose larger nearby cities too
- Prepare for travel
- Book accommodation early

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many exam centers can I choose for NEET 2026?

You can choose up to 4 exam city preferences while filling the application form.

### What if I don't get any of my preferred centers?

NTA will allot you a center in a nearby city within the same state or nearby state.

### Can I change my exam center after submission?

Yes, during the correction window (usually 1 week) you can change center preferences if seats are available.

### How do I know my exact exam center?

Your exact exam center and address will be mentioned on your admit card, available 2-3 weeks before the exam.

### Is there any fee for changing exam center preference?

During the correction window, there may be a nominal fee for making changes.
