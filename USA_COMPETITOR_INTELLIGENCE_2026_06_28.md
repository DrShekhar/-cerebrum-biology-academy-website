## 0. THE STRATEGIC HEADLINE

Across ~35 US competitors in 5 segments, **not one is a biology-only specialist with medical-faculty credentials.** Every player is either (a) a multi-subject generalist (Kaplan, Princeton Review, Wyzant, Varsity), (b) a self-paced content/AI tool (Khan, Fiveable, UWorld, Revision Village), or (c) a solo/alumni-run niche shop (Biolympiads, Baology). This leaves four open lanes for Cerebrum:

1. **The "biology specialist" identity** — nobody owns "the biology experts" across AP/MCAT/IB/USABO.
2. **AIIMS-faculty credential** — uniquely credible for MCAT Bio/Biochem, USABO Semifinal, IB HL (all test degree-level human physiology/biochem/molecular — exactly AIIMS-track depth). Competitors use med-students, IBO alumni, or anonymous "former teachers."
3. **The mid-premium price gap** — commodity ($0–40/hr) and prestige ($155–395/hr) ends are crowded; the **$60–130/hr specialist sweet spot is thin** (only undifferentiated Varsity sits there).
4. **Structured live + accountability** — self-paced tools have <20% completion; failed self-studiers are warm leads. Live cohorts with a known faculty face are uncontested in several niches.

## 4. PRICING POSITIONING RECOMMENDATIONS (to validate, not auto-apply)

- **AP Biology**: a **spring exam-prep cohort at $99–199 (6–8 wks)** and a **semester small-batch at $299–599** occupy an empty band between Fiveable ($79/yr) and private tutoring ($48–132/hr). 1:1 at $60–90/hr undercuts Ivy Bound/Revolution with a stronger credential.
- **MCAT Bio/Biochem**: position as a **section specialist** (not a full MCAT course) at **$60–90/hr** — 40–70% below Blueprint/Princeton tutoring; target weak-in-bio premeds, IMGs, retakers, NRI.
- **USABO**: current ~$4,500/yr + $90/hr is premium vs Biolympiads ($1,900/60h ≈ $32/hr) — **justify via faculty credential** or add a lower-priced cohort tier to compete on the Open-exam entry funnel.
- **IB Biology**: $90/hr sits at the low end of the qualified-teacher band ($80–130); the AIIMS credential **supports $110–130/hr**. Add a productized **IA mentorship package** (no competitor has one live).
- **Universally**: add a **score-improvement / money-back guarantee** — Kaplan, Princeton, Blueprint, Altius all have one; it's table stakes for head-to-head comparison.

## 5. CONTENT / SEO GAPS competitors leave open
- **Free lead magnets** competitors use: AP score predictor (Fiveable/UWorld), free diagnostic, free CARS passages (Jack Westin), free weekly IB Zoom (Revision Village). Cerebrum has none of these as top-of-funnel hooks.
- **Biolympiads owns "how to prepare for USABA" SEO**; Clastify owns "IB Biology IA examples." Cerebrum's pages exist but under-marketed in prep communities (Reddit r/Biolympiads, AoPS, Discord).
- **Marco Learning's site was DOWN during research** — their AP boot-camp / FRQ-checklist content niche is momentarily open.
- **Most-missed AP topics** (Fiveable data): proteins (45% miss), non-Mendelian genetics (45%), experimental design/SIVS — target explicitly in content.
- **Barron's is book-only** (not a course) — worth clarifying in any comparison page so prospects don't conflate it with live coaching.

## 6. WHAT NOT TO DO
- Don't fight Blueprint/Kaplan full-MCAT courses or Altius mentoring on their terms (capital/content-heavy) — be the **bio/biochem specialist complement/alternative**.
- Don't fight UWorld on QBank volume ($339 / 3,000+ Qs is a commodity price).
- Don't fight Khan ($0) or Revision Village ($249) on price for self-paced content — compete on **live + accountability + specialist depth**.
